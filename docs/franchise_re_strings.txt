0x82351102	The TimeStretch PlugIn uses a time domain based approach to altering the length of a sound. This is in contrast to frequency domain based approaches that must first convert the signal to the frequency domain (typically via the FFT). Both approaches have advantages and disadvantages associated with them, and there is no single ideal time stretching method that works well on all types of audio content in all processing situations. This particular time stretcher is designed to work well in the stretching and contraction of speech content, typically with stretch ratios in the range of 0.75 to 1.5. In contrast to a frequency domain based approach, it achieves this with less or no chorusing and phasing artifacts, no transient smearing, and a much lighter processing load.
0x8235140B	The basic mechanism used in achieving a stretch or contraction involves analyzing the sound waveform and determining successive locations at which a high degree of correlation exists. With the knowledge of these high correlation points, entire sections between these points can be looped to achieve a stretch, or cut to achieve a contraction. The correlation analysis operation is by far the most processor-intensive part of the stretching algorithm, and has been optimized to use as few cycles as possible. The user has some control over how this algorithm is used via several construction parameters, so that cycle usage or efficiency can be traded off against functionality and quality. These are discussed next.
